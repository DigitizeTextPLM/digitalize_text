When he saw Trelawny's printed letter, Lord Sidney wrote to Douglas Kinnaird sayingthat it was incorrect throughout. He had no sooner heard from Count Gamba andFletcher that Byron would have wished his body to return to England than that coursewas 'immediately carried into effect' - not in spite of himself and Sir Frederick Stoven,but with their perfect concurrence, while 'General Adam was at Corfu the whole timeand never interfered in the slightest degree about the matter'.
