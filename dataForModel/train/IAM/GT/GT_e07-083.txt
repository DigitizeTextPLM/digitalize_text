Every garden boy will agree that having to wash clay pots is a definite factor infavour of their abolition; in fact one young man was so wholehearted about this a fewyears ago that he made the headlines for having smashed a large number to relievehis feelings. On the small-to-medium establishment, it is a disagreeable chore whichnobody tackles with any enthusiasm, whereas on larger establishments the pots canbe sterilized or washed by machine.
