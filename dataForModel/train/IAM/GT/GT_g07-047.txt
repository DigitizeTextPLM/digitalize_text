She had earned the right to be left in peace. Byron's fame was, of course, verywonderful, but it carried with it too many reminders of his terrible indiscretions - thewriting of Don Juan, which she had never ceased to deplore, his shocking blasphemieslike the Vision of Judgement, his making friends with the atheist known to her as 'thatinfamous Mr. Shelley', and his mixing with really low and horrid people such as thesubversive journalist Leigh Hunt, whom one would never conceivably meet in decentsociety.
