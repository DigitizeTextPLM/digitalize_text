But there is heart in the telling, and an intense realism in the situation. A younggirl lives in a single dingy room with her slatternly, promiscuous mother. In suchsurroundings she learns sex is something sordid, and when she experiences it for thefirst time herself it is incoherently, clumsily, but half shyly and half inquisitively. Asis the case in Fanny her first lover is a sailor who leaves her to bear his child and sailsaway.
