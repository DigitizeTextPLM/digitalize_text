Mr. Thorneycroft, the Minister of Aviation, who arrives in Bonn tomorrow for talkswith the Federal Government on a European space satellite project, will find theGermans interested in the principle of space research, but rather sceptical aboutBritish plans for organizing it. Stated more bluntly, they are still unconvinced thatthis is not primarily an effort on Britain's part to salve Blue Streak, which wasabandoned last summer as a military project; or that the new European space satelliteis indeed to be purely scientific in character.
