Forever associated with Charles is Archbishop Laud, now so much nobler a figure thanformer historians led us to believe. Laud was enthusiastically hated by Calvinists andPuritans, and the sentiment was mutual. But Laud was no Romanizer. One of hisfirst public acts was a hard-hitting battle with the Jesuit, Fisher. But anyone whowas friendly with James and Charles, the Puritans argued, was necessarily a menace.
