Trebling the rate was arrived at by a simple arithmetic which argued that, as thefirst poll-tax had supplied 22,000, a tax of three times the rate would produce 66,000.The only concession made in view of the objections to the first two poll-taxes was thesuggestion that the rich should help the poor - but this was only a pious hope becauseno machinery was provided for carrying it into effect, and a subordinate clause wentfar to nullify what small effects it had - no man and wife together were to pay morethan twenty shillings, a restriction which applied to the generous rich as well as tothe mean.
