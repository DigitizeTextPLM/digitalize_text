At one and the same time, therefore, occur the beat of the apex, the thickening of theheart walls, and the forcible expulsion of their contained blood by the contraction ofthe ventricles. Going on in Chapter Three to the movement of the arteries, likewisegauged from the dissections of living animals, Harvey noted that contraction of theheart and the apex beat occur in systole, simultaneously with dilatation of the arteriesand of the artery-like vein, and expulsion of the ventricular content.
