There were few things in Augusta's whole life, full of calamities though it was, thathurt her more than this contemptuous slight from George Anson Byron, whom she hadloved with an unswerving loyalty, and had looked on as her intimate friend. Moreover,he was without the right to make such pronouncements: he had inherited nothingfrom her brother but his title, whereas she was not only of nearer consanguinity butthe chosen recipient of his property.
