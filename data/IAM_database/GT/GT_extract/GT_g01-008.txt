Obedient to the policy of his King, de Vienne avoided trouble at sea as cleverly as DuGuesclin avoided it on land. Gaunt was compelled to give up his search for an elusivefoe, and, afraid to return home without something to show, he foolishly attemptedto besiege the well-protected fortress of St Malo. This involved the dreary method ofmining operations in which Gaunt, under the Black Prince, had shown considerableskill at the siege of Limoges.
