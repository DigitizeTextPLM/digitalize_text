She had loved Byron, but she had betrayed him, betrayed him not twice, as he hadbetrayed her, but again and again over a long span of time, fawning on his implacablewife, purveying to her in secret the unguarded letters he never suspected any eye buther own would see, feeding the stealthy fires of her animosity: and having betrayedhim, she had grown to fear and almost to hate him.
