Catherine looked at the books lying around his room, and asked if she might borrowthe stories of Isaac Babel to read on the train. Catherine is 13. I suggested she mightfind them difficult, but she said, 'Philip reads them, doesn't he?' During the journeyI read newspapers and watched her pretty frowning face as she turned the pages ofBabel, for she was determined to let nothing get between her and her ambition to beworthy of Philip.
