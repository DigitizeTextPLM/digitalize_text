The sparkling society of the Doria Palace, the flattery of Orsini's obsession, thethwarted ambitions of her restless spirit, all threw into sharp relief the contrast ofher grey life with the splendid one that filled her dreams. At the Villa Montalto shefelt an alien, and although she had repeatedly urged Francesco to give her a separateestablishment, he invariably pleaded his financial dependence on his uncle, who hadbuilt the villa for his family.
