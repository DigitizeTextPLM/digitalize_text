3. On 23rd November, 1959, we submitted an Interim Report. This dealt withtwo questions which arose from our terms of reference and which had been broughtspecially to our notice. First, we were asked to examine the risks attending the abuseof carbromal and bromvaletone and preparations containing these substances. ThePoisons Board had already considered this problem but, in the absence of sufficientevidence that these compounds were widely abused, had not recommended them forcontrol as &quot;poisons&quot; under the Pharmacy and Poisons Act, 1933.
