There was always the spice of an orgy in those Roman feasts, for instance, with alltheir peacocks and nightingales' tongues; unreasonable surfeit, too, in the elaboratefashion of eating brought out of Italy into France, we are told, by Catherine de Medici.The peasant in those days, as ever, ate sparingly, but generously enough in his ownfashion, save at feast times, when he, too, let himself go; and it was from his simplerfood that the later renaissance of cooking was to come.
